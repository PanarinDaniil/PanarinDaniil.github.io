Technical FAQs
What makes 878TEN unique?

878TEN is the only Universal Personal Telecommunications (UPT) number range in the world and one of only two global number ranges that has been given an ENUM delegation by the ITU (the other range has just 100,000 numbers). As a result, 878TEN is the only E.164 number range where an individual or organisation can actually own the telephone number (as opposed to it being owned by a government or a regulator). There are some country codes that have been granted an ENUM delegation, however, these provide “second service” ENUM, rather than the “first service” ENUM of 878TEN. In a second service ENUM, the ENUM benefits can only be accessed via the Internet, with the number behaving as a normal telephone number if it is accessed from a telephone network. 878TEN is a “first service” ENUM delegation, so it always functions as an ENUM enabled identifier, even when called from a telephone network.

ENUM is the protocol that allows E.164 numbers to address internet elements which use DNS records to associate Universal Resource Indicators (URIs) with an 87810 number. In this way, an 87810 number can be used to address any digital entity including people, devices and entities. The application of 878TEN as a UDI has limitless applications but some of the industries that stand to benefit the most are FinTech, HealthTech, RegTech and IoT.

Third parties can request to connect to specific elements and the individual chooses whether or not to let them. For example, you may want your doctor to be able to see certain health records but you probably don’t want a social media site to see them. Where the owner of an 878TEN UDI grants permission to a third party, information is always provided in an encrypted format, removing the need for the user’s personal data to be stored in a single place.

878TEN works simultaneously across both IP and telephony worlds and includes a corresponding IMSI. This means 878TEN can integrate with multiple service providers to deliver many different end-to-end IoT solutions. Because 878TEN numbers are truly global, a device such as a washing machine that is factory-fitted with an 878TEN IMSI will be able report errors, download software and feed back usage data to the owner, the manufacturer or a data house wherever it ends up in the world. 878TEN can be dialled from any Asterisk-based VoIP platform and also supports the deployment of low cost narrowband technologies, such as SIGFOX or LoRaWAN.

Furthermore, each 87810 UDI has been assigned an MCC/MNC pair enabling SIM cards to be issued and roaming agreements to be made. In the not very distant future, your 878TEN number (which can be the +87810 version of your current phone number), will be able to make and receive calls and access data anywhere in the world and will be the only phone number you will ever need. Another unique feature of +87810 is that it is the only Universal Personal Telecommunications (UPT) range issued by the ITU within the E.164 international numbering plan. UPT defines global personal mobility, independent of a SIM or a device. This allows the UPT numbers to be controlled by an individual or an organisation rather than a telecommunications operator, regulator or government body.

E.164 number ranges are part of the International Public Telecommunications Numbering Plan that is administered by the International Telecommunication Union (ITU). The ITU is the United Nations (UN) agency for information and communication technologies (ICTs). Among its responsibilities, the ITU assigns global radio spectrum and satellite orbits, develops the technical standards that ensure networks and technologies seamlessly interconnect, and allocates and manages the global telephone numbering system.

The 878 country code is a permanently delegated number resource for the purposes of Universal Personal Telephony (UPT) and enjoys an ENUM delegation, a protocol that unifies the telephone system with the Internet. 878 10 becomes the first and only telephone number range providing worldwide portability for voice, email, SMS and web services.
